About

Biography

A journey from Prayagraj to world stages, carrying the soul of Indian classical music through the saxophone and bamboo flute.

Born in a musician family of Prayagraj, Priyank Krishna inherited music from his illustrious father Pt. Ram Krishna, a prominent clarinet player of the country. He began his formal training in Hindustani classical music at a very young age and has since developed a unique voice on the saxophone and bamboo flute.

In his concerts, Priyank explores the melodic richness of the Gayaki ang, while using the tonal possibilities of the saxophone to present tans, meends and intricate improvisations. His performances have been appreciated by audiences, connoisseurs and music critics alike.

Priyank is an A-Grade artist of All India Radio in Hindustani classical music, recipient of the Government of India Scholarship for Young Artistes, and has served as an academic counsellor (Flute) with IGNOU, New Delhi.
